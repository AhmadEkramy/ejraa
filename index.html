Frequently Asked Questions

Specialized clinics are complexes that include several clinics in different specialties, allowing patients to visit and receive consultations in any of them. A medical center, on the other hand, is similar to a small hospital but dedicated to only one specialty. It is permitted to have a major operating room, an emergency department, an intensive care unit, inpatient rooms, recovery rooms, and a complete oxygen network.

Private Clinic: No specific size requirements, but it must include a room with a desk for medical consultations, a place for hazardous medical waste, and a sterilization area. Specialized Clinics: A consultation room must be provided for each specialty, along with a place for hazardous waste and a dedicated sterilization room. Medical Center: Each inpatient bed or ICU bed must have an area of at least 8 square meters. The facility must also have a gas network room, a sterilization room, a hazardous waste room, a major operating room starting from 20 square meters, a recovery room, and an intensive care room.

General Instructions for Medical Facilities

1. Examination Room Equipment

  • Examination bed according to specialty
  • Privacy screen (curtain separator with iron base) for patient privacy during examination
  • Good natural ventilation
  • Desk and comfortable chairs for doctor and patient
  • Tools specific to the medical specialty

2. Medical Waste and Sterilization Areas

  • Every licensed medical facility must contain a designated place for medical waste
  • Presence of a room or equipped place for sterilization, equipment varies according to specialty type or facility

3. Handling Medications Within the Facility

  • Medications are allowed in small quantities and for personal use only according to work needs
  • Storing medications in large quantities for sale purposes is prohibited to avoid being considered drug trafficking

5. Safe Medication Storage

  • Store medications in suitable places away from heat or humidity
  • Clearly write opening date and expiration date on packages

6. Safety and Health

  • Commitment to safety procedures to protect both staff and patients
